Bloom Mind is a student-focused mental health organisation offering peer support, educational resources, workshops, and safe spaces for open conversation tailored to student life.

Bloom Mind is open to all students—regardless of background, field of study, or age—who want support, resources, or community focused on mental wellbeing.

Bloom Mind began at Rosebank College, Braamfontein. We run in-person activities on campus and offer online services accessible to students from other campuses as well.

We operate as a student-led initiative supported by volunteers and advocates. We are in the process of any necessary formal registration (where applicable) to expand our reach.

Our services are designed specifically for students. While we may partner with non-student organisations, primary access is for current students.

We offer one-on-one peer support, group support circles, workshops & webinars, a wellness resource library, student interaction spaces, crisis referrals, and more.

Peer support sessions pair you with a trained student supporter who listens, helps you explore coping strategies, and points you to further resources if needed.

You can discuss anything affecting your wellbeing: stress, anxiety, study pressure, relationships, loneliness, identity concerns, or personal challenges.

Yes—workshops and webinars provided by Bloom Mind are free for students.

We are not a crisis counselling service. However, we provide crisis support information and can quickly refer or connect you with professional helplines and emergency contacts.

Sessions can be booked through our website booking form or in person during campus hours. Booking details are on the Sessions/Services page.

We encourage registration to reserve a place and plan materials, though some workshops may accept limited walk-ins depending on space.
